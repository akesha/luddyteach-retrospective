What does a teaching blog look like from above?

LuddyTeach began as a collection of practical, research-informed posts for computing and STEM educators. Over 77 posts and more than 26,000 words, the blog covered course design, assessment, AI integration, student engagement, and faculty wellbeing.

This retrospective applies six analytical methods to the full corpus, revealing patterns that aren't visible when reading posts one at a time: which themes dominate, how the conversation shifted over time, which ideas travel together, and which posts stand apart from the rest.

01 The Seven Themes How the corpus distributes across categories, and what 15 cross-cutting dictionaries reveal. 02 Streams of Change How the blog's focus shifted over time, visualized as a streamgraph. 03 The Landscape Every post projected into two dimensions via PCA. Click to explore thematic DNA. 04 Alliances & Rivalries Which themes travel together and which rarely share a post. 05 Thematic Diversity Shannon entropy measures how broadly each post spreads its energy. 06 Posts That Broke the Mold The posts that combined themes in ways the blog rarely did. 07 Data & Methods Transparency about analytical choices, plus downloadable data.
