What does a teaching blog look like from above?

LuddyTeach began as a collection of practical, research-informed posts for computing and STEM educators. Over 77 posts and more than 26,000 words, the blog covered course design, assessment, AI integration, student engagement, and faculty wellbeing.

This retrospective applies six analytical methods to the full corpus, revealing patterns that aren't visible when reading posts one at a time: which themes dominate, how the conversation shifted over time, which ideas travel together, and which posts stand apart from the rest.

Every analytical choice is documented in the Methods section. The data is available for download.

The Seven Themes

Each post belongs to one of seven categories assigned during writing. Here's how the corpus distributes across them.

Teaching Strategies and AI & Technology together account for more than half the corpus, reflecting the blog's dual focus on pedagogical practice and technological change.

What Did LuddyTeach Talk About?

Beyond the seven editorial categories, every post was scored against 15 cross-cutting keyword dictionaries. These dictionaries capture concepts that cut across categories — a Teaching Strategies post might score high on scaffolding, metacognition, and active learning all at once. The dictionaries fall into four conceptual clusters.

Technology Tools leads by a wide margin, reflecting the blog's roots in a computing education department. Assessment Design and Equity & Inclusion follow, anchoring the blog's dual commitment to rigorous evaluation and inclusive practice. Metacognition and Active Learning trail — not because they're absent, but because their vocabulary is more diffuse.

How Broad Was Each Post?

Some posts spread their energy across many themes (high diversity). Others concentrate on just one or two (low diversity). Shannon entropy captures this in a single number — a measure of how evenly a post's thematic energy is distributed.

Each dot is a post. The red line shows the moving average. Higher means more thematically diverse.

Data & Methods

Transparency about how we got here.

The Corpus

77 blog posts from LuddyTeach, a teaching resource site for CS faculty. Each post includes full text, category, and metadata. Total corpus: 26,374 words.

Thematic Scoring

Each post was scored against 15 keyword dictionaries covering cross-cutting pedagogical concepts (AI tools, active learning, assessment design, metacognition, equity & inclusion, and 10 more). Scores use term frequency normalized by post length, weighted by inverse document frequency (IDF) to emphasize distinctive rather than common terms.

Principal Component Analysis

The 77 × 15 scoring matrix was z-score standardized, then projected to two dimensions via PCA. The first two components capture the axes of greatest variation in the blog's thematic profile.

Correlation Analysis

Pearson correlations computed between all 105 theme pairs. Significance tested at p < 0.05 with a minimum |r| > 0.15 threshold.

Thematic Diversity (Shannon Entropy)

Each post's theme scores are normalized to a probability distribution. Shannon entropy H = −Σ p·log&sub2;(p) measures how evenly scores spread across themes. Normalized to [0, 1] where 1 = maximum diversity.

Novelty Scoring

Composite of two measures: (1) Euclidean distance from the corpus centroid in z-score space, and (2) mean pairwise cosine distance to all other posts. Both normalized to [0, 1] and averaged.

Streamgraph

Posts binned into bi-monthly periods by publication date. Category counts stacked using D3's wiggle offset for visual clarity.

Tools

Analysis: Python (pandas, scikit-learn, scipy). Visualizations: D3.js v7. Site: static HTML/CSS/JS.
